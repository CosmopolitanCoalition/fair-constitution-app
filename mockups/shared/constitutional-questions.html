Stage-0 stub The five seed entries are anchored and summarized below so citation markers have a live target from Stage 1; the full prose treatment lands with Stage 6.

1 · Factions → polymorphic endorsements

Touches: Art. II §2 · Establish Proportional Voting Systems — Art. II §2 · Ensure Election Security and Integrity — Art. II §4 · Committees (three clauses) — Art. III §2 · Composition and Selection of Executive Committees

What the build surfaced: the running experiment removed the faction layer entirely. STV satisfies factional-makeup matching at the individual-preference level with no party layer; observer/audit standing transfers to endorsing organizations and candidates.

Implemented answer: a universal organizations model (political_party | business | nonprofit | common_good_corp | informal) plus polymorphic endorsements — any organization or individual can endorse any candidate; members with no endorsing organization are first-class.

Candidate for next draft

2 · Committee tie-break — normalized-quota vote share

Touches: Art. II §4 · Committee Seat Assignment Process

What the build surfaced: the constitutional tie-break ("each members own 1st choice vote performance in the prior election") assumes raw 1st-choice counts are comparable — but STV quota transfers make them incommensurable across winners.

Implemented answer: ties go to the seat holder with the largest share of votes after normalizing quotas — preserving the proportionality the STV election produced and one-person-one-vote, with no faction layer.

Candidate for next draft

3 · Legislature sizing — the cube-root law

Touches: Art. II §2 · Establish Independent Election Boards (min 5 / max 9 + subdivision)

What the build surfaced: the constitution bounds a chamber (5–9) but is silent on how many population-apportioned seats a parent legislature should have in total.

Implemented answer: legislature_sizing_law (v1: cube_root only) — total seats = max(5, round(∛ Σ direct-children population)). Future alternative laws reserved.

Candidate for next draft

4 · Districting — composites of child jurisdictions

Touches: Art. II §2 · Establish Independent Election Boards ("drawn equally, contiguously, and fairly") — Art. II §8 · Subdivision of Legislatures

What the build surfaced: free-form line-drawing fights community integrity and auditability.

Implemented answer: districts are Webster-apportioned composites of direct child jurisdictions, each within the constitutional 5–9 seat range; oversized ("giant") children recurse into their own sub-district budgets. Leaf giants — above the ceiling with no subdivisions — still require manual line-drawing: the open frontier.

Candidate for next draft

5 · Open-ballot two-phase elections

Touches: Art. II §2 · Ensure the Right to Vote and Stand for Election — Art. I · Right to Vote / Right to Stand for Office

What the build surfaced: a continuous, filterable approval phase (opening the moment the prior election certifies) with a finalist line (top X per race, X = f(seats) · CLK-21) makes the right-to-stand guarantee operational at planetary candidate counts — while write-in of any validated candidate preserves the right absolutely.

Implemented answer: two-phase elections: continuous approval → finalist cutoff → time-boxed ranked window → one-count PR-STV (Droop quota, Gregory fractional transfers) filling all seats per district.
