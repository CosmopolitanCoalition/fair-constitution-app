District mapper — United States legislature, scoped to New York

Districts are composites of direct child jurisdictions — whole counties grouped together, never split lines on a map. Seats are Webster-rounded inside the constitutional 5–9 range; a child that exceeds the 9-seat ceiling subdivides further, carrying its own sub-district budget down the chain.

Cube-root sizing: total seats = max(5, round(∛ population)) — the chamber grows with population but never without bound. Webster rounding then splits a parent's budget across its districts so every seat represents the same number of people.
