Board elections — Bluefin Logistics

Three counts seat one board: shareholders elect the owner track by STV, workers elect the worker track by STV, and then the entire board jointly elects its chair by ranked choice. At 740 workers, Bluefin's co-determination ratio is 9 owner seats and 3 worker seats.

Owner track — 9 seats by STV

1,204 shareholder ballots · 12 candidates · Droop quota floor(1,204 ÷ (9 + 1)) + 1 = 121.

STV — single transferable vote: surplus votes above the quota transfer fractionally (Gregory) to next preferences, so every ballot keeps working. Droop quota — the smallest total that mathematically guarantees no more than nine winners.

Worker track — 3 seats by STV

692 of 740 workers voted · 5 candidates · Droop quota floor(692 ÷ (3 + 1)) + 1 = 174. The worker track exists because Bluefin crossed the 100-worker threshold (CLK-13); its 3 seats come from the uniform scale toward parity at 2,000 (CLK-14) — see the scaling meter.

Joint chair — single winner by RCV, entire board votes

All 12 seated board members — owner-elected and worker-elected alike — rank the nominees. Majority of the full board required: 7 of 12. Three board members were nominated.
