Organizations · work councils

Co-determination scaling

When an organization employs 100 or more workers, its workers elect board members. The first worker seat arrives at 100 workers, and worker representation scales uniformly until it reaches parity with owner-side seats at 2,000 workers. The same scale binds private enterprises, Common Good Corporations, and executive departments.

Scales uniformly between the first seat (100) and parity (2,000) · Art. III §6

Parity means worker-elected seats equal owner-elected seats. "Scales uniformly" is read plainly: seats grow proportionally between the two constitutional endpoints, in even steps, so no headcount jump is ever rewarded or punished. The worker count is simply everyone signed on through the worker form (Worker registration · F-IND-014) — recurring labor counts regardless of organization type. The owner side runs on the share system; in a Common Good Corporation the board of governors stands where shareholders would.

Composition change → joint chair election

Every time the scale adds (or removes) a worker seat, the board's composition changes — and a composition change triggers a fresh joint chair election by the entire board, owner-elected and worker-elected members voting together. The board is valid only while its composition matches the scale.

Applies equally — private enterprises, CGCs, and executive departments

The scale does not care who owns the entity. Owner-side seats are shareholder-elected on a private board, appointed governors on a CGC or executive department board. Art. III §6
