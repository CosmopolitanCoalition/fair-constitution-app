Reading the registry

  • Type is the scheduler contract: recurring intervals re-arm on fire; countdowns expire once; bounded windows open and close; thresholds watch a quantity and fire on crossing.
  • Amendable means a valid legislative act can change the default within fixed bounds — see amendments and the settings register.
  • Fires names the workflow the scheduler invokes; each link opens the step-by-step walkthrough.
  • Every fire event is appended to the audit chain.

Glossary: a "peg quorum" counts all serving members, not those present; the Droop quota is the smallest vote count that mathematically guarantees a seat; the finalist count X is a function of seats in the race.

About this surface

What this screen does: the scheduler itself is the engine behind term synchronization and 90-day meeting enforcement; the Fires column links every other consumer.

Entity state machine: clocks are stateless trigger records — they move other entities (Election, Emergency Powers, Vacancy, Residency Claim) through their lifecycles.
