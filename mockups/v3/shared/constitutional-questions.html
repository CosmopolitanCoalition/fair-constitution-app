Each entry records the constitutional text touched, what the attempt to build it surfaced, the implemented answer, and a status badge tracking it toward the post-game redraft. Amendments to the running system itself go through the two Art. VII doors — this page is the honest margin note beside them.

1 · Factions → polymorphic endorsements

Touches: Art. II §2 · Establish Proportional Voting Systems — Art. II §2 · Ensure Election Security and Integrity — Art. II §4 · Committees (three clauses) — Art. III §2 · Composition and Selection of Executive Committees

What the build surfaced: the running experiment removed the faction layer entirely. STV satisfies factional-makeup matching at the individual-preference level with no party layer; observer/audit standing transfers to endorsing organizations and candidates.

Implemented answer: a universal organizations model (political_party | business | nonprofit | common_good_corp | informal) plus polymorphic endorsements — any organization or individual can endorse any candidate; members with no endorsing organization are first-class.

Where to see it: the open ballot filters by endorsing organization or individual endorsements with no party column anywhere; the committee surfaces show proportionality staying legible through endorsements alone. Observer and audit standing during counts transfers to endorsing organizations and the candidates themselves.

2 · Committee tie-break — normalized-quota vote share

Touches: Art. II §4 · Committee Seat Assignment Process

What the build surfaced: the constitutional tie-break ("each members own 1st choice vote performance in the prior election") assumes raw 1st-choice counts are comparable — but STV quota transfers make them incommensurable across winners.

Implemented answer: ties go to the seat holder with the largest share of votes after normalizing quotas — preserving the proportionality the STV election produced and one-person-one-vote, with no faction layer.

Where to see it: the assignment run on the committees surface — each member's normalized vote share is displayed beside the tie-break so the math is auditable on sight.

3 · Legislature sizing — the cube-root law

Touches: Art. II §2 · Establish Independent Election Boards (min 5 / max 9 + subdivision)

What the build surfaced: the constitution bounds a chamber (5–9) but is silent on how many population-apportioned seats a parent legislature should have in total.

Implemented answer: legislature_sizing_law (v1: cube_root only) — total seats = max(5, round(∛ Σ direct-children population)). Future alternative laws reserved.

Where to see it: the district mapper derives every seat budget from the sizing law and displays the quota (population ÷ seats) beside it. At Earth scale the law yields roughly 2,000 population-apportioned seats — a number the constitutional text never had to confront.

4 · Districting — composites of child jurisdictions

Touches: Art. II §2 · Establish Independent Election Boards ("drawn equally, contiguously, and fairly") — Art. II §8 · Subdivision of Legislatures

What the build surfaced: free-form line-drawing fights community integrity and auditability.

Implemented answer: districts are Webster-apportioned composites of direct child jurisdictions, each within the constitutional 5–9 seat range — built from real jurisdictional lines wherever possible. Children above the seat ceiling subdivide further, carrying their own sub-district budgets; where a jurisdiction exceeds the ceiling with no child subdivisions in the dataset, district lines must be drawn manually — the open frontier.

Where to see it: the district mapper renders draft / active / archived plans with Webster-rounded seats, contiguity and integrity indicators, and the optimal / suboptimal / current grouping stats; election-board observation runs through the board console.

5 · Open-ballot two-phase elections

Touches: Art. II §2 · Ensure the Right to Vote and Stand for Election — Art. I · Right to Vote / Right to Stand for Office

What the build surfaced: a continuous, filterable approval phase (opening the moment the prior election certifies) with a finalist line (top X per race, X = f(seats) · CLK-21) makes the right-to-stand guarantee operational at planetary candidate counts — while write-in of any validated candidate preserves the right absolutely.

Implemented answer: two-phase elections: continuous approval → finalist cutoff → time-boxed ranked window → one-count PR-STV (Droop quota, Gregory fractional transfers) filling all seats per district.

Where to see it: the open ballot (the finalist line, revocable approvals, jockeying deltas) and the ranked ballot with its always-available write-in of any validated candidate.

Candidate for next draft

6 · Countback without factions

Touches: Art. II §5 · Countback Procedure for Filling Vacancies

What the build surfaced: the countback procedure leaned on the faction layer — and with factions replaced by polymorphic endorsements (entry #1), the faction-scoped parts of the procedure no longer mean anything.

Implemented answer: countback runs universally — the prior election's ballots are re-run with the vacated member removed as a candidate, with no faction filtering anywhere in the procedure. Failure still falls through to a special election within 90–180 days (CLK-04).

Where to see it: the countback engine view and flow WF-ELE-03.

7 · Bicameral dual agreement — the per-kind threshold

Touches: Art. V §3 · Independent Agreement for Legislative Actions — Art. II §2 · Peg Quorum Requirement to Full Membership

What the build surfaced: Art. V §3 requires both kinds of members to agree independently but names no per-kind threshold or quorum. The intent is to preserve the equal-parts and population-proportional parts over the existing boundaries of the nation-state system, with dual agreement required.

Implemented answer: each kind must meet its own peg quorum and pass independently — a majority of ALL serving members of that kind (supermajority where the act type requires it). Needs resolving in the constitutional text.

Where to see it: the dual-agreement meters on bill detail (bicameral toggle).

How an entry lands here

A question qualifies when the constitutional text under-determines a mechanism the software must decide — and the build picks an answer. The entry quotes only the citation labels of the text touched, states the implemented answer in one paragraph, and links the surfaces where the answer is visible. Every · as implemented marker elsewhere in the mockups resolves to its anchor here (#q1–#q7).
