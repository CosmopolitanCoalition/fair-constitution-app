# Currency & economic operations in *A Fair Constitution*

You asked me to check the constitution for currency-based workflow operations you
may have missed. This is the full sweep of every economic, monetary, fiscal, and
agreement operation the Template actually authorizes, read from
`docs/extracted/fair_constitution.md`. Each maps to a v2 economic surface.

The headline: the Template says **less** about money than you might expect and
**more** than is obvious. It names currency, worth, measurement standards, taxes,
fees, borrowing, shares/fair-market, joint resources, and contracts — but it is
**silent on UBI, salaries, and dues** (those are `[POLICY]`, amendable variables you
are adding, which is exactly right). The danger isn't over-reach; it's missing the
few hard rails and the powers that are easy to overlook.

---

## 1. Individual economic rights (Article I) — the demand side

| Clause | Operation | v2 surface |
|---|---|---|
| **Economic Freedom** | "engage in enterprise, commerce, and other economic activities freely" | marketplace, org registration |
| **Free Movement of Capital and Goods** | "move capital, goods, and services within and between Jurisdictions" | marketplace, transfers, cross-jurisdiction trade |
| **Access to Common Good Services** | "equal access to goods and services of the Common Good… except jurisdictional association" | CGC listings on identical terms |
| **Freedom to Contract** | "freely form contracts and agreements with others to protect their rights and interests" | **instruments of agreement** |
| **Supremacy of Rights** | "free from any law, **contract, or agreement** that attempts to disempower A Fair Constitution or their rights" | **the constitutional floor on every agreement** |

> **Easy to miss #1 — the contract floor.** Supremacy of Rights means **no contract
> clause can waive a constitutional right.** Every instrument of agreement in the app
> must carry this floor: a labor contract can't sign away your vote; a joint-ledger
> agreement can't gate a civic right. The mockup renders this on every agreement.

---

## 2. The hard rails (Article II) — what money can NEVER do

| Clause (Art. II) | Rail |
|---|---|
| §8 **Prohibition of Compulsory Payments for Civic Rights** | "Individuals cannot be compelled to pay **taxes, fees, liens, or costs** to exercise their Civic Rights and Obligations." **HARDENED.** |
| §2 **Maintain Population Records** | records kept "for the purposes of **apportioning taxes**, resources, and legislative seats" — taxation is tied to population apportionment |
| §9 **Creation of Treasury Departments** | "for the purpose of **managing the capital and finances** of their Common Good" |

> **The no-paywall rail is the single most important economic constraint.** It is
> why the stipend is a payment *to* people and never a payment *required of* them,
> why no marketplace fee can attach to a civic-right form, and why dues are a private
> voluntary matter. Every economic surface renders this as a structural impossibility.

## 3. Organizations & the supply side (Article III)

| Clause (Art. III) | Operation | v2 surface |
|---|---|---|
| §4 **Boards of Governors** | "Governors execute the charters, acts and rules of their departments" — the BoG **disburse** treasury funds | treasury disbursement |
| §5 **Common Good Corporations** | CGCs "provide goods and services"; **treated identically** to private enterprise | marketplace (CGC badge) |
| §5 **Acquisition of monopolistic / available enterprises** | "Legislatures may **purchase** Private Enterprises that gain monopolistic control… as well as… **for sale on the Open Market**" | public-service journey, stock |
| §5 **Fair Market Compensation** | "**Shareholders**… are paid **at least the fair market price for their shares** upon conversion" | **stock / shares** |
| §5 **Reorganization or Dissolution** | "reorganize, dissolve, or **sell** Common Good Corporations **on the Open Market**" | org settings, the Open Market |
| §5 **Public Domain IP** | CGC patents/secrets/copyright "universally and eternally in the public domain" | CGC IP register (v1) |
| §6 **Work Councils** | workers "**contracted to perform work**" → worker-elected board seats; chair "elected jointly by the entire Board" | co-determination, agreements (labor) |

> **Easy to miss #2 — shares and fair-market valuation exist.** The only place the
> Template ties money to ownership is **shares** (Art. III §5). Stock, a fair-market
> price, and the Open Market are all named. Your stock-management surface is on solid
> constitutional ground; the valuation matters because monopoly acquisition must pay
> *at least* fair market.
>
> **Easy to miss #3 — "the Open Market" is a constitutional term**, used twice. The
> marketplace isn't just a feature; it's the venue the constitution names for buying
> and selling enterprises.

## 4. Jurisdictional economic powers (Article V) — the big one

### Art. V §2 — shared & indivisible resources (the joint-ledger basis)
> "Jurisdictions control natural resources and other assets within their boundaries
> while **jointly sharing resources that flow through or exist indivisibly between
> multiple Jurisdictions**."

> **Easy to miss #4 — this is the constitutional basis for JOINT LEDGERS.** When a
> resource (a harbor, a watershed) exists indivisibly between jurisdictions, they
> *jointly share* it — which means a **joint-controlled account whose movements
> require the agreement of the co-owners.** Your "joint controls between agreeing
> users and organizations of ownership of ledgers" is exactly this clause (for
> jurisdictions) plus Freedom to Contract (for users/orgs).

### Art. V §4 — Joint Powers (taxes, borrowing, IP, infrastructure)
| Clause | Operation |
|---|---|
| **Imposition and Collection of Taxes and Fees** | "impose and collect **taxes, fees, and charges** to **settle debts** and ensure… well-being" |
| **Borrowing Funds** | "Jurisdictions **borrow funds based on their credit**" |
| **Acquisition of Land and Capital** | "purchase the necessary lands and capital **at a fair market value**" |
| **Promotion of Progress in Science and the Arts** | "offering creators and innovators **exclusive rights** to their works for designated durations" |

> **Easy to miss #5 — borrowing & debt.** Jurisdictions can **borrow on their credit**
> (Art. V §4) and taxes settle **debts**. So the economic model includes a **credit/debt
> instrument**, not just spending. The treasury surface renders borrowing as a gated
> facility.
>
> **Easy to miss #6 — IP as exclusive, time-limited rights.** Creators get "exclusive
> rights… for designated durations" (Art. V §4) — a tradeable property right — *except*
> CGC IP, which is perpetual public domain (Art. III §5). IP is an economic instrument
> with two opposite regimes.

### Art. V §5 — Reserved Powers (CURRENCY — reserved to the root)
> "The most encompassing Jurisdiction… reserves the power to **produce and regulate
> currency, determine its worth, and define standards for measurements** necessary for
> regulating industry and commerce."

> **This single clause is the entire monetary constitution.** Three powers, all
> root-reserved and HARDENED:
> 1. **Produce & regulate currency** → issuance, monetary policy.
> 2. **Determine its worth** → valuation / the worth basis (a dual-door lever).
> 3. **Define standards for measurements** → **the unit and its subdivisions.**
>
> **Easy to miss #7 — unit subdivision is "define standards for measurements."** Your
> "ability to subdivide units internally" is constitutionally the measurement-standards
> power: the root defines the unit (1 ç) and its subdivisions (centunits, milliunits).
> A constituent jurisdiction can't mint its own currency or redefine the unit — that's
> reserved.
>
> Also here: "**oversee extra-jurisdictional trade and relationships**" — cross-border
> trade oversight, the basis for the trade-talk live room.

### Art. V §7 — Union: "codify **Amendable Variables**"
On forming/joining a union, jurisdictions codify their amendable variables — which is
where the monetary keys (worth basis, issuance, stipend rates, the economic clock)
get pinned. UBI rates and currency params are amendable variables, set per instance.

## 5. What the constitution does NOT say (so it is `[POLICY]`, and you're right to add it)

- **UBI / a civic stipend** — no clause. It is a policy floor you add, grounded in
  residency (Art. I) + Common-Good management (Art. V §2) + Treasury (Art. II §9).
  Eligibility = residency only (the same absolute gate as voting).
- **Salaries / compensation for office** — **completely silent.** The only money-and-
  office text is fair-market share compensation on monopoly conversion (Art. III §5,
  unrelated). So paying operators / office-holders / moderators is `[POLICY]`, which is
  why it must be a **differential, not a salary** (a salary risks making pay a
  qualification for office, colliding with the absolute Art. I right to stand).
- **Member dues** — silent; a private agreement between a member and a voluntary
  organization (Art. I freedom of association + contract), never a civic-right gate.
- **The economic clock / disbursement interval** — silent on cadence; it is the
  amendable `ubi_period_days` sweep, a dual-door monetary key.

> **The governing principle for all of §5:** the app provides the **governance and
> transparency machinery** and takes **no economic position** — how big the stipend is,
> whether it's inflationary, what the tax rate should be is "the job of a legislature
> and a Judiciary to come together and find a system that works." Every number is an
> **amendable variable on the public record with its enacting act**, changed only
> through the dual-door lever (F-LEG-031), never an admin knob.

## 6. The currency workflow operations, end to end (the checklist)

1. **Define the currency** (root): name, worth basis, **unit + subdivisions** · Art. V §5
2. **Set monetary policy** (root, dual-door): issuance rate, inflation target, worth · Art. V §5
3. **Run the economic clock**: the `ubi_period_days` sweep → the stipend run (F-TRE-004)
4. **Disburse the civic stipend**: base (residency floor) + role differential (capped) · `[POLICY]`
5. **Raise revenue**: levies & fees, apportioned via population records · Art. V §4 / Art. II §2
6. **Borrow on credit**: a debt facility · Art. V §4
7. **Enact a budget** → spawn appropriations → BoG disburse · Art. III §4
8. **Keep the public ledger**: double-entry, append-only, hash-chained
9. **Trade on the Open Market**: listings ↔ orders, offers ↔ requests · Art. I / Art. III §5
10. **Form agreements**: labor, ownership transfer, joint ledgers, sale — each with the
    Supremacy-of-Rights floor · Art. I
11. **Hold & move joint ledgers**: agreement-gated movements · Art. V §2 + Art. I
12. **Manage shares**: ownership, fair-market valuation, conversion · Art. III §5
13. **Collect dues / file taxes**: private, voluntary or apportioned, never a civic-right gate · Art. II §8
14. **Co-determination**: a labor contract can cross the worker-seat threshold · Art. III §6
15. **Cross-jurisdiction trade & IP**: oversight at the encompassing jurisdiction;
    exclusive rights vs. CGC public domain · Art. V §4–5 / Art. III §5

Every one of these has a v2 surface (built or Planned). The seven "easy to miss" items
above — the contract floor, shares/fair-market, the Open Market as a named venue, joint
ledgers from shared resources, borrowing/credit, IP exclusive-rights, and unit
subdivision as measurement standards — are the ones worth double-checking against your
own design intent.
