Executive home

Every executive starts as a committee delegated by the legislature. A supermajority — plus a supermajority of constituent jurisdictions where they exist — can convert it to a directly elected office, as either a committee of five or more equal officers, or a single individual with four advisors. Executive terms always equal the legislative term.

Delegated by default · conversion by supermajority · terms locked to the legislative term.

Protected by the constitutional test suite A supermajority is two-thirds of all serving members — counted against everyone serving, never just those present.

About this surface

This screen shows how an executive office is set up and how it can change shape: the delegated committee, its conversion to an elected office, and later modification by a supermajority of constituent jurisdictions. Orders and oversight live on executive actions; departments on the department registry.

How the office moves: it starts as a committee delegated by the legislature, can convert to an elected office — either a committee of five or more equal officers, or a single individual with four advisors — and can later be modified or wound down. The strip inside each model panel highlights the live state.
