Link your ID

Submit a government ID where your jurisdiction can check it automatically.

Which IDs can be checked is decided by each jurisdiction's legislature.
Sent encrypted for a simple yes/no match — the number itself is never stored here.
Submitted — the ID office checks it and reports back a simple yes or no. Your account works fully while this is pending. Continue to say where you live — your rights never wait on this step.

In-person option

This jurisdiction can't check IDs automatically yet. A local officer can confirm yours in person instead — a short appointment with a neutral officer of your jurisdiction.
  1. Request an appointment below — the queue is served by the jurisdiction's administrative office.
  2. Bring any identifying document recognized under local law.
  3. The officer records a simple 'confirmed' — no document data enters the system.

Skipping this entirely is always allowed — unverified accounts keep every right that residency grants.
