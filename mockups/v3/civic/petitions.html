How a petition travels

Two ways a petition can stop: the signatures fail their check, or the law is found unconstitutional.

Signatures are checked by the independent election board; the constitution check is done by the courts.

About this surface

This screen lets anyone who lives in a jurisdiction draft and register a petition, sign open petitions, and follow a petition through its stages. The constitutionality review stage is handled by the judiciary.

Each petition moves through the stages shown in the strip above; each list row badges its current stage.

Signing is open to anyone who lives at the petition's scale — you don't need to have started a petition to sign one.
