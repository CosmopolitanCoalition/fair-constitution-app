Constitutional challenge tracker

Any inhabitant can challenge a law that unjustly impedes their rights. When the court finds a contradiction, three resolution paths open: the legislature amends, the legislature overrides by supermajority within the veto window, or — if the window closes with neither — the judiciary edits the law directly.

A finding, a recommended fix, a reasonable timeframe to act, and an override window — all on a public clock.

About this surface

What this screen does: it tracks an inhabitant's challenge to a law from filing through the court's finding to whichever of the three resolution paths closes it out. Hearings, the amendment bill, and executive enforcement each live on their own screens, linked from here.

How a challenge moves: Filed → Heard (full court if it's a major question) → Finding and remedy issued → Legislative window open → amended by the legislature, overridden, or remedy applied by the judiciary → Law edited → Closed. Opinions remain commentary on the law as written or edited.
