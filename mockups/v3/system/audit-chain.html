How the chain works

Each entry stores the hash of the previous entry alongside its own payload hash — hash(n) = H(hash(n−1) ∥ payload(n)). Altering any historical entry breaks every hash after it, so tampering is detectable by anyone who recomputes the chain. Ballot and residency identities are never written to the chain in the clear; only commitments are chained.
