One shared timeline (the "lockstep")

All three elected branches in the New York County stack run on the same term, certified 2030-11-01 → expiring 2035-11-01. Appointed officers run on their own 10-year clocks and are shown for contrast — appointment terms are deliberately decoupled from election cycles.

Stylized stand-in — production renders this timeline from the live term registry.

Places switch on at different times as their player populations reach critical mass, so their clocks start staggered — a county's first term can begin years before its neighbors'. As neighboring places grow, their election days converge toward one shared general-election day. Ending together is the end-state, not the starting condition.

One clock, every trigger

The general election interval is the single source: legislative terms define it, elected executive and elected judicial terms equal it, and the scheduler derives every election trigger from it. Officials never touch the calendar.

Vacancies never reset the clock

A countback or special-election winner serves the remainder of the term — New York County's seat 4 replacement will still face the voters on 2035-11-01 with everyone else. Special elections fit inside the shared term; they never move it.

What the engine refuses to do

  • Skip, delay, or reschedule a derived election — there is no way to do it; the trigger fires from the term clock regardless of who holds office.
  • Extend any elected term past the common expiry, including under active emergency powers — elections cannot be disrupted.
  • Let executive or judicial terms drift from the legislative term when an office converts to elected.

Attempted violations are rejected before they take effect and recorded · audit chain

About this surface

This screen shows how every elected term expires together and how each election is scheduled automatically from one shared clock. The term synchronization engine fires the general cycle along with the executive and judicial elections from the same derived schedule.

An election moves through stages — certified last cycle, approval phase open, and so on, through to certified. The lockstep clock is what moves every race into the approval phase at once.
