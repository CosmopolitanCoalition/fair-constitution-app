System of record

Amendments

The constitution changes through exactly two doors. The settings it leaves open move by ordinary legislative acts, inside locked bounds. The locked core itself (the hardened layer) moves only by a new release of the software, made in the open with every constitutional check passing publicly. Nothing changes silently, and nothing changes any other way.

Door two — the hardened layer

The supermajority floor

The supermajority fraction is amendable, but its definition has a hardened floor: no redefinition may produce a threshold below majority + 1 of all serving members. The default is two thirds, computed as ceil(serving_members × 2/3) — against members serving, never members present.

New York County chamber: 9 serving → majority 5 · floor (majority + 1) = 6 · supermajority ceil(9 × 2/3) = 6

Supermajority, glossed: the share of every seated member — vacant seats still count in the denominator until refilled — so absence can never lower the bar.

The proportionality ratchet

The voting method (proportional voting with the Droop quota) is listed as amendable — but only in one direction: it may be replaced exclusively by a more proportional method. The engine rejects any act that would adopt plurality, first-past-the-post, or any method scoring lower on the proportionality tests.

About this surface

What this screen does: it shows the two ways the constitution can change — amending the constitution — where the settings door runs through the amendable setting change and the ordinary bill flow, and every change is sealed in the audit chain.

Where settings live: amendable settings are scoped per jurisdiction; ratification climbs the nesting chain through constituent supermajorities.

In engineering terms: the hardened layer is a set of protected files guarded by the constitutional test suite — a release deploys only when the full suite passes, and the release is sealed in the audit chain.
