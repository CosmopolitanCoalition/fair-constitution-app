The chamber

A circular chamber, like the emblem — no head of the room, no government benches facing an opposition. Members sit most senior to least senior in alternating seats, so experienced and new members work side by side as natural staggering occurs.

Gold ring = Speaker (politically neutral, votes only to break ties). Dashed = vacant seat in countback — vacancies join at the junior-most position. Seniority is total days as a chamber member; ties (common — a general election seats everyone at once) break by normalized prior election performance. Larger chambers add outer circles, up to 12 seats in the innermost.

Quorum: 5 of 8 serving (5 of 9 when the chamber is full) — supermajority: two-thirds of all serving, rounded up = 6 of 8

About this surface

What this screen does: shows the chamber seat map, the member roster, the term countdown, the current seat-4 vacancy, and the checklist this legislature completed after certification. The Speaker is chosen by supermajority at the first session; a vacant seat hands off to the vacancy handling flow.

Behind the scenes: each committee seat moves from allocated to seated; a vacancy moves from detected to declared to countback-running to filled — that is the state behind seat 4.

Endorsements, not parties: the roster shows endorsing organizations because people and organizations endorse candidates directly — there's no party machinery, and members with no endorsements have exactly the same standing (ledger #1).
