The Speaker

The office and the person: Yuki Tanaka chairs the New York County legislature. The Speaker is politically neutral, votes only to break ties, and presides over removal proceedings — except their own case. Elected by supermajority ranked-choice vote at the first session; replaceable the same way.

About this surface

What this screen does: it describes the office of the Speaker — the neutrality rule, the tie-break record, the member-priorities channel, and the rule for presiding over removals — with the full machinery in the drawer above. Running a session lives on the session console; electing or replacing the Speaker happens at the first session of a term; removal proceedings run on the ethics & removals page.

How a motion moves: the Speaker recognizes, schedules, and (only on ties) decides; everything else is recorded against the member who moved it.
