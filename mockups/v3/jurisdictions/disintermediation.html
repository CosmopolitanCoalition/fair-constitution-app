Disintermediation — dissolving an intermediary jurisdiction

An intermediary layer dissolves only when every constituent jurisdiction agrees and the encompassing jurisdiction consents. Its acts do not vanish — they are incorporated into each former constituent's law, with conflicts surfaced and resolved before the dissolution takes effect.

All constituents agree, and the encompassing jurisdiction agrees, before the acts are incorporated into the constituents.

About this surface

This screen walks through dissolving an intermediary jurisdiction: each constituent and the encompassing jurisdiction vote, the intermediary's acts are merged into each former constituent's law (with conflicts surfaced and resolved), and the topology updates. The mirror-image growth path is union formation.

Once dissolved, the intermediary is gone; its former constituents remain self-governing throughout.
