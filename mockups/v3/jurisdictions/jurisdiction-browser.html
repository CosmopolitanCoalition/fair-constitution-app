About this surface

What it does: shows population records and apportionment for a place, and is the entry to the district mapper via "View Legislature & Districts". Every place on Earth, planet to neighborhood — its boundaries, its people, and who represents them. The map is real, preloaded geography (about a million places); the live count of verified residents layers on top. Every place governs itself — sitting inside a bigger one changes scope, never rank.

What the view shows: breadcrumb ancestors, natural level badge, population (WorldPop year) beside live member count, region & dataset provenance, the maps-accepted / apportionment-completed record, and the federation authority line. Map layers: Names · Population · Members · Raster (WorldPop dots).

The containment mirrors what's built: the map fills every pixel inside a thin wrapper; zoom-out is bounded so the world always fills the view, and east–west panning wraps seamlessly with no visible seam. On a phone, the map takes the top of the screen and the panel scrolls beneath it. The shipped Jurisdiction viewer mounts exactly here and adopts this design system.
