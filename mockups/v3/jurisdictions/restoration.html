Constitutional restoration

If a government is countermanded, captured, or destroyed, the constitution does not pause — restoration cascades through three tiers until a legitimate government stands again. Each tier activates only when the one above it cannot act. This page is the standing drill.

Detection triggers a restoration elections cascade.

Activation conditions

Restoration mode arms when any one of three conditions is detected for a jurisdiction's government. Detection is evidence-based and judicially reviewable — never a unilateral switch.

Three conditions: countermanded, captured or disabled, or destroyed.

The restoration cascade

    Restoration elections reuse the bootstrap election machinery — system-run, STV/Droop, constitutional defaults (bootstrap sequence).

    Legitimacy scoring

    When more than one body claims to be the government, legitimacy is assessed against three criteria — the same tests the Template applies to all authority.

    • Minimize consent violations — fewest people governed without their consent.
    • Balance interests uniformly — no constituency privileged over another.
    • Govern effectively — actually able to execute the constitutional order.

    Legitimacy criteria — authority by consent.

    Defensive forces

    Defensive forces are bound to protect the most legitimate government — the claimant scoring highest on the criteria — not the incumbent, not the strongest, not their own chain of command's preference.

    Defensive forces protect the most legitimate government.

    During restoration, elections, sessions, and courts that still function cannot be disrupted — the same hard guarantee that bounds emergency powers.

    Where restoration sits in the Jurisdiction lifecycle

    Restoration-Mode is a branch, not an end state — the jurisdiction returns to Self-Governing the moment a restored government is seated and certified.
