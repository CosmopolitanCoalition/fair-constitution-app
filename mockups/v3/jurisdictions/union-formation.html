Union formation — compatibility, codification, ratification

Two or more independent jurisdictions form a union by analyzing where their amendable variables and institutions diverge, codifying one shared set, and ratifying by supermajority — of the applicant population and of the union's constituent jurisdictions. Exit mirrors entry.

Variable codification workspace

For each divergent variable the founding act codifies one value for the union. Constituents keep their own values for their own scope where the power is joint.

Ratification meters

Union formation requires a supermajority of individuals in each applicant population and a supermajority of constituent jurisdictions already in the union (none yet — this is a founding union of two). Denominators are whole populations, never just those voting.

Supermajority of applicant individuals + supermajority of union constituents · Art. V §7 · supermajority = ceil(N × 2/3) · Art. VII

Join and exit mirror each other

Joining an existing union runs the same entrance-clause process: the applicant aligns its institutions, then admission passes on a supermajority of the applicant's individuals plus a supermajority of the union's constituent jurisdictions. Departure requires the same supermajorities — no one-way doors.
