Manhattan Water & Power

A Common Good Corporation is a public enterprise chartered by a legislature to provide goods or services to its jurisdiction. It competes and is regulated exactly like its private peers — with one permanent difference: everything it creates belongs to everyone.

Executive oversight

Oversight is assigned to the New York County Public Works & Utilities department, whose Board of Governors receives this CGC's periodic reports. Oversight does not exempt the CGC from any regulation a private utility would face — same inspectors, same courts, same standards.

Co-determination state

The 100 → 2,000 worker scale applies to CGCs exactly as it does to private enterprises and executive departments. At 1,450 workers, the board carries 5 worker-elected seats beside its 7 appointed governors, and the chair is elected jointly by the entire board.

Public-domain IP register

Everything this CGC creates is public domain from the moment of creation — never patented, never enclosed, and never reclaimable by a future private buyer (see conversion rules). A permanent, protected rule.

Reorganization, sale, or dissolution

Only the legislature can reorganize, sell, or dissolve this CGC — by vote, through the transfers & conversions surface (the CGC Reorganization/Sale Vote). A sale to a private buyer moves the enterprise, never the work already in the public domain.
