Emergency powers

Emergency powers exist for exactly two causes — natural disaster and actual invasion. They require a supermajority, run at most 90 days, are judicially reviewable at any time, auto-expire, and can never disrupt elections, sessions, or courts. Any active power is the first order of business at every session.

The hard rails

  • Civic-process protection: elections, sessions, and courts cannot be disrupted — enforced in code.
  • Auto-expiry at the declared duration — no action required, no extension without a fresh supermajority.
  • First order of business: an active power leads the agenda of every session until it ends.
  • Judicially reviewable at any time (WF-JUD-06).
