Mecklenburg County judiciary

An independent, appointed court serving Mecklenburg County and its constituent jurisdictions. Created by supermajority act of the county legislature; judges nominated by the constituent jurisdictions in equal numbers and confirmed by legislative consent vote.

Appointed is the default judiciary type · Art. IV §2 — conversion to elected requires dual supermajorities · Art. IV §3

How this court was created

Judiciary Creation Act 2027-02 — passed 7 of 9 serving members (supermajority required: 6 of 9) · Mecklenburg County legislature · effective 2027-09-01.

Supermajority: two thirds of all serving members — counted against everyone holding a seat, never just those present.

ceil(serving × 2/3) = 6 of 9 · Art. VII — scope: county law and below · Art. IV §2

Nomination — equal numbers from every constituent jurisdiction

Each of the seven constituent jurisdictions of Mecklenburg County nominated exactly one candidate — equal numbers are mandatory. Mint Hill declined to nominate, so the legislature's judicial committee supplied the nomination in its stead (the constitutional fallback).

Constituent jurisdictions nominate equal numbers; judicial committee as fallback · Art. IV §2

Conversion to an elected judiciary

Converting this appointed court to a directly elected one needs two independent supermajorities: the county legislature, and the constituent jurisdictions themselves. Neither alone is enough.

If conversion passes, judges are thereafter elected in groups of at least 5 via STV, and judicial terms sync to the general election clock.

STV: single transferable vote — a proportional ranked-choice count where surplus votes transfer fractionally so no ballot is wasted.

Supermajority of legislature + supermajority of constituent jurisdictions · Art. IV §3 — groups of at least 5 per race · CLK-15 · Art. IV §4 — terms synced · CLK-10

Term length — judicial appointments

10 years per judicial appointment Set at founding · must stay in lockstep with civil appointments (also 10 years) · CLK-09 · CLK-10 · Art. IV §4; Art. II §9

Judicial and civil appointment lengths move together — a legislative act changing one changes both. Renewals re-run the nomination and consent process (WF-JUD-07).
